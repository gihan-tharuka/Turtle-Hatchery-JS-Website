Entanglement in fishing gear

It’s estimated that the fishing industry contributes to the death of thousands to tens of thousands of sea turtles each year. Turtles that become trapped in longlines, gill nets and trawls are thrown away as bycatch. And those that manage to avoid fishing nets are impacted by the disruption to their food supply and habitat.

How to prevent...

  • Using Turtle excluder devices (TEDs)
  • Using Biodegradable and less harmful fishing gear
  • Implementing time and area closures in fishing grounds
  • Training programs and workshops for fishermen

Natural Predators

In nature, sea turtles face a host of life and death obstacles to their survival. Predators such as raccoons, crabs and ants raid eggs and hatchlings still in the nest.Once they emerge, hatchlings make bite-sized meals for birds, crabs and a host of predators in the ocean. After reaching adulthood, sea turtles are relatively immune to predation, except for the occasional shark attack.

How to prevent...

  • Predator exclusion or deterrent methods
  • Nest relocation
  • Nest monitoring and protection
  • Hatchling disorientation reduction

Illegal Sea Turtle Shell Trade

Hawksbill sea turtles, recognized for their beautiful gold and brown shells, have been hunted for centuries to create jewelry and other luxury items. As a result, these turtles are now listed as critically endangered.While illegal trade is the primary cause of this decline, the demand for shells continues today on the black market.

How to prevent...

  • Trade controls within consumer and supplier states
  • Educating tourists and suppliers
  • International cooperation and agreements
  • Raising awareness about illegal shell trade

Human consumption

Although sea turtles have spiritual or mythological importance in many cultures around the world, this has not prevented humans from consuming their eggs or meat. In many coastal communities, especially in Central America and Asia, turtles have provided a source of food. People will wait until the female has deposited her eggs to kill her. People use other parts of them for products, including the oil, cartilage and skin.

How to prevent...

  • Legal protection and enforcement
  • Ecotourism based on sea turtles
  • Monitoring and reporting systems
  • Alternative protein sources and livelihoods
